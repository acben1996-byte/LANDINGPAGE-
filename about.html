About ARIO

Independent hotels deserve institutional-grade revenue science. Not a watered-down version of someone else's system.

ARIO exists because the hospitality software market has a gap. Enterprise vendors sell to chains and price independents out. The independent-tier vendors price right but oversimplify the math. Nobody was building proper RM software for the independent hotel that runs Opera Cloud and takes revenue management seriously.

Why ARIO exists

A market structurally underserved

01
Enterprise vendors built for chains
Duetto, IDeaS, and the dimensional-depth tier price independents out — six-figure annual licences, six-month implementations, dedicated data-science teams to operate them. Built for properties that have a CFO signing the cheque.
02
Independent-tier vendors oversimplify
The £200/month tier prices right but treats every hotel as a 30-room B&B with one room type and no group business. The maths is shallow, the audit trail is non-existent, and the "AI" is usually a moving average.
03
The middle is where the value sits
Boutique 50–250 room properties running Opera Cloud or Mews. Real revenue managers. Real comp sets. Real group business. Real ownership pressure. Nobody was building proper RM software for them — so we did.
"
A hotel makes pricing decisions worth millions every year. The tools it makes them with should be honest about what they know — and disciplined enough to refuse a recommendation they can't justify.
ARIO operating principle, since day one
Who ARIO is built for

Independent. Boutique. Owner-operated.

If you're running 50–500 rooms, have a real revenue manager (or a GM who plays one part-time), and want institutional-grade decisions without an institutional contract — you're our customer. We're not for property aggregators or 1,000-room flagship chains; that's enterprise vendor territory.

Independent
Single-property operators and boutique portfolios running Opera Cloud, Mews, or Cloudbeds. Want institutional-grade revenue management without enterprise-tier procurement.
Boutique groups
3–15 property collections with a head office RM team. Need chain features (cross-property roll-up, OHIP write-back) without a chain price tag.
Branded properties
Franchise operators inside larger flag who run their own revenue function. ARIO complements brand-mandated tools without replacing them.
Boutique hotels
Owner-operated properties where the GM is also the RM and accountant. Want decisions explained in plain language they can defend to ownership.
How we operate

Three principles that don't change.

These principles drive every product decision, every pricing tier, every line of code. They're how a one-person shop today scales into a chain-grade vendor without losing what made it different.

Honest defaults

Every "—" in the dashboard is a system telling the operator the truth: this metric isn't calibrated yet. No fabricated numbers. No "industry default" hiding behind a confidence percentage. If we don't know, we say so.

Audit-grade by construction

Every decision is content-hashed. Every push leaves a tamper-evident row. Every override carries operator email and timestamp. We don't ship features that bypass the audit chain — including for ourselves.

Operator intent wins

Per-date constraints, kill switch, shadow mode, founder release — all exist so the operator's decision overrides anything ARIO computes. The system serves the RM. Never the reverse.

Our commitments

What we promise every operator

Whatever the tier, whatever the property size, whatever the integration depth — every customer gets the same operating commitments. These are not aspirations; they are how the platform is built.

Same-day response

Every inbound — sales, support, security review, integration — gets a substantive response from London the same business day. No ticket queues, no auto-responders, no offshore handoffs.

Audit-traced by construction

Every recommendation, every override, every rate publish is logged in a tamper-evident journal. You can reconstruct any decision the platform has ever made — for ownership, for compliance, for your own peace of mind.

Honest defaults

If we don't have enough data to calibrate a number, we show "—" — not a fabricated default dressed up as a confident estimate. The platform refuses to push a recommendation it can't justify.

No vendor lock-in

Your decision journal, forecasts, and historical data export at any time, in any format. If you ever want to leave, you take everything with you. No hostage data, no exit fees, no commercial friction.

Operator intent wins

Every guardrail you set is respected. Every override you make is honoured. AutoPilot only runs inside the boundaries you define. The system serves the revenue manager — never the reverse.

Continuous discipline

Models retrain nightly. Forecasts back-test against your actuals every morning. Calibration drift triggers an alert. The platform earns its accuracy claim — every day, against your real data.
