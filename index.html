The problem
Revenue management
is still mostly manual

The average revenue manager spends 20 hours a week on tasks that should take 5. Rate checks, competitor shopping, RFP calculations, forecast reports — all done by hand, in spreadsheets, across multiple tools that don't talk to each other.

Meanwhile, the market moves. Competitors reprice. Demand signals shift. And the window to act closes before you've finished your morning data pull.

45 minutes every morning on rate checks
Manually pulling data from hotel PMS, OTAs, and competitor sites before making a single pricing decision.
Group RFPs take 45–90 minutes each
Displacement calculations done in spreadsheets, with no real data on what transient demand you'd be giving up.
Competitor data without context
You can see their rates — but not whether they're panicking, yielding up, or following a deliberate strategy.
Institutional knowledge lives in people's heads
When a revenue manager leaves, so does every decision, pattern, and insight they built up over their tenure.

Our mission

Institutional-grade revenue intelligence,
built for independent hotels

For decades, the rigour of quantitative finance — ensemble forecasting, willingness-to-pay modelling, scenario simulation, audit-traced decision-making — has been reserved for the largest chains and the most expensive enterprise tools.

ARIO exists to close that gap. We're building the Revenue Operating System that an independent hotel, a boutique group, or a branded operator can run end-to-end — without a six-figure annual licence, a six-month implementation, or a data science team to babysit it.

Every line of the platform — from the forecast layer to the decision journal to the OHIP write-back — is built around one principle: a revenue manager should never have to defend a decision they cannot reconstruct.
